![Corinne Chin Portrait](/img/cc.jpg)

Corinne Chin is an <a
href="http://st.news/disappearingdaughters" target="_blank"><u>Emmy-winning</u></a>
journalist and newsroom leader with a proven record of identifying and
developing diverse talent on a global scale. As Director of Video Talent
at The New York Times, she leads recruitment and development as the
newsroom continues to expand its video journalism across the depth and
breadth of the report: producing on-the-ground reporting, pioneering
Visual Investigations and video podcasts that showcase the expertise of
Times journalists.

She was previously the Director of News Talent for recruitment at The
Associated Press, where she led newsroom hiring, oversaw the Global News
Internship Program and designed training and development initiatives to
support the next generation of AP leaders.

Before moving into leadership, Corinne built her career as a video
journalist and producer at AP, <a
href="http://st.news/under-our-skin" target="_blank"><u>The Seattle Times</u></a> and <a
href="https://www.cnn.com/2021/10/08/americas/hawaii-sign-language-extinction-as-equals-intl-cmd/index.html" target="_blank"><u>CNN,</u></a>
publishing award-winning documentaries and digital video stories
recognized by the Emmys, Pictures of the Year International, Edward R.
Murrow Awards, Society for News Design, Online News Association, Los
Angeles Asian Pacific Film Festival and Seattle Asian American Film
Festival. She has also served as a contest judge for the OJAs, POYI,
CPOY and the Society for News Design. Her reporting has taken her around
the world, from Nairobi to Rio de Janeiro to <a
href="http://st.news/disappearingdaughters" target="_blank"><u>Ciudad Juárez,</u></a>
and her experience in the field continues to shape the way she mentors,
recruits and supports journalists today.

Deeply committed to equity and inclusion, Corinne has developed
newsroom diversity initiatives, delivered trauma-informed safety
training and spoken at major journalism conferences. She is a graduate
of leadership programs through Poynter, the News Leaders Association
(ASNE), the Maynard Institute and the International Women’s Media
Foundation, and she pays those lessons forward through regular teaching
and mentorship for Report for America, Next Gen Journalism and the Asian
American Journalists Association.

Corinne is available for public speaking and training on video
storytelling, career development, leading through influence, covering
civil unrest, digital security and the importance of representation and
belonging. Want to talk through any of these topics 1:1? You can sign up
for a coaching call at <a
href="http://digitalwomenleaders.com" target="_blank"><u>digitalwomenleaders.com.</u></a>
